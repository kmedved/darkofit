## M6 tripwire and supersession

M6 v2 is internally coherent but no longer has forward ranking authority. Its
rule requires wins in at least 60% of cells, and its known-negative replay is
the selector whose count-gate verdict this audit retracts. Leaving it active
would make the new pipeline reproduce the old mistake automatically.

The create-only `m6-quality-successor-v3` contract therefore:

1. preserves the same ten-dataset, medium, 60-cell paired execution grid;
2. removes win counts and arbitrary minimum-effect ranking;
3. gates only aggregate non-harm (`≤1.000`), worst-dataset harm (`≤1.020`),
   and leave-one-dataset-out concentration (`≤1.003`);
4. uses combined B1+B2 as the surviving known advance;
5. uses native ordinal C2 as the surviving known kill because its worst-task
   ratio is `1.317510`; and
6. replays the old selector as an explicit tripwire that must now advance.

These thresholds are development triage, not a ship rule. M6 v3 can spend and
rank a mechanism inspection; it cannot change defaults, expose an API, or
replace Tier-D confirmation.

## Scope and method

The sweep covered every terminal mechanism, policy, certification, funding,

placement, or lockbox evidence was accessed.

## Standing rule

Whenever a gate class is retracted or materially reformed, the retraction
record must enumerate every historical disposition that depended on it,
classify each victim under current policy, and assign a new-identity
re-adjudication or an explicit healed, superseded, or still-valid disposition.

**A retracted rule with unexamined victims is an unfinished retraction.**
