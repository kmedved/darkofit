# DarkoFit SynthGen adoption protocol

## Purpose and boundary

This is the I2 adoption gate for a synthetic mechanism probe. It asks whether
an independently generated, contamination-safe tabular suite can reproduce at
least seven of nine already-recorded DarkoFit decisions. Passing makes the
suite eligible only for mechanism direction-finding in Tracks L, C, and S.
It can never promote a parameter, policy, preset, or default.

The protocol was frozen from clean published DarkoFit `main` at `b915d75`.
No SynthGen model outcome may be inspected before the generator, corpus,
suite, ledger, slices, splits, estimands, and thresholds below are committed.

## Source and license

The generator is a modified port of ChimeraBoost 0.15.0 commit `851ab7f`,
`benchmarks/synthgen/`, licensed under Apache-2.0. Ported files must identify
their modification, and DarkoFit's `NOTICE` must record the source commit and
scope.

The port remains benchmark-only and NumPy-only. It does not become a DarkoFit
runtime dependency or public API.

## Contamination-safe calibration corpus

The donor's reduced `corpus_marginals.json` is inadmissible because dataset
identities were discarded before DarkoFit's CTR23 exclusions could be proved.
Rebuild the corpus from a newly fetched or retained raw OpenML listing:

1. fetch OpenML study 457 solely to exclude every TabArena dataset ID;
2. load all 35 OpenML dataset IDs and normalized names from the committed
   `ctr23_suite_snapshot.json`;
3. exclude those IDs and names before version deduplication, curated tagging,
   sampling, or marginal reduction;
4. retain only aggregate marginal rows in the checked-in snapshot, but record
   the source timestamp, exclusion-source hashes, requested and observed
   exclusion counts, and a hash of the excluded identities;
5. fail closed if study 457 cannot be fetched or if any declared CTR23
   identity is absent from the raw listing without an explicit recorded
   reason.

The generator version must use a DarkoFit-specific namespace. Any change to
the corpus or generator bumps that version and regenerates golden hashes and
frozen suites. No CTR23 or TabArena target, split, score, prediction, or model
artifact may be read.

## Generator checks

The port must preserve deterministic independent RNG streams, SCM ancestry
rules, categorical/entity construction, missingness, exact generative Bayes
floors, content hashing, frozen smoke/screen/full nesting, row budgets, and
earned canaries. Tests must cover deterministic bytes, global NumPy RNG
isolation, target dependence, feature/target shapes and dtypes, nondegenerate
classes, floor consistency, suite nesting, and golden hashes.

Canaries are earned only by a predeclared three-seed ceiling check after the
DarkoFit-specific version is frozen. Canary IDs live outside generated
metadata. Empty or outcome-selected canary slices fail adoption.

## Common benchmark design

- Frozen screen suite, regression tasks only for the nine ledger decisions.
- Three deterministic split seeds: `17`, `29`, and `43`.
- Per seed, a target-blind 75/25 train/test split. Classification is used only
  for the separate canary/floor check and is stratified.
- The DarkoFit model random state equals the split seed for every regression
  arm. This keeps stochastic arms reproducible and paired without sharing a
  mutable RNG.
- Every arm sees identical generated bytes and split indices.
- DarkoFit uses six total CPU threads per fit and no GPU.
- Common regression budget: 600 rounds, depth 6, no early stopping or refit,
  and `diagnostic_warnings="never"`.
- Parameters not named by an arm remain at the current public default.
- Numeric and mixed categorical matrices use the generator's native
  representation and declared categorical indices.
- Prediction must be finite and have exactly one scalar per test row.
- A failed fit, missing coordinate, duplicate coordinate, imputation,
  non-finite metric, source change, or dataset-hash mismatch fails adoption.

For each dataset and arm, form the geometric mean of paired
`RMSE_arm / RMSE_control` across the three seeds. Slice aggregates are
geometric means across equally weighted datasets. A win has ratio below
`1 - 1e-9`; a loss has ratio above `1 + 1e-9`.

Bayes-normalized excess RMSE is report-only because near-floor denominators
can be unstable. Fit time, prediction time, peak RSS, and model size are
diagnostic and cannot rescue a ledger disagreement.

## Frozen metadata slices

All slices exclude saturated cell-rule datasets unless stated otherwise.

- `ordinary_regression`: all nonsaturated regression datasets.
- `noisy_nonlinear`: regression, `noise_level >= 0.25`,
  `interaction_depth >= 2`, and dominant function not `linear`.
- `smooth_linear`: regression with dominant function `linear`.
- `categorical_regression`: regression with at least one categorical feature.

Every decision slice must contain at least eight frozen screen datasets.
Slice membership and counts are emitted before any model fit.

## Separate canary/floor check

This clause was made explicit before the first ledger fit. It resolves the
original phrase "canary/floor check" without changing a ledger arm, slice, or
threshold.

- Earned membership and the baseline floor check come from the committed
  complete freeze artifact. Every `CANARIES` id must have three finite raw
  verifier values and must satisfy the thresholds frozen in
  `synthgen/filters.py`.
- The no-variance check uses every categorical canary in the frozen screen
  suite. The slice must be nonempty.
- For each such canary, compare `ts_permutations=4` with the
  `ts_permutations=1` control over the verifier's stratified split seeds
  `0`, `1`, and `2`.
- Both sides use the fixed verifier configuration: 2,000 maximum rounds,
  learning rate 0.1, depth 6, L2 3, 128 bins, CatBoost tree mode, forced
  ordered boosting off, validation fraction 0.2, early-stopping patience 50,
  no refit, and six CPU threads.
- Score sum-form Brier loss. For each dataset, average candidate minus control
  across the three seeds; then average those deltas across equally weighted
  datasets. The no-variance check passes only when this final mean is at most
  `1e-9`.

This classification check is an adoption alarm, not a tenth ledger decision.
It cannot turn a ledger disagreement into an agreement.

## Nine-arm DarkoFit decision ledger

1. **Student-t location versus RMSE.** Historical source:
   `basketball_robust_heads_result.md`. Agreement requires ordinary-regression
   ratio `>= 1.001` and more dataset losses than wins. The Student-t arm uses
   its required `tree_mode="lightgbm"`; DarkoFit rejects this vector-valued
   distributional head in CatBoost tree mode.
2. **MAE versus RMSE.** Same source and requirement as arm 1.
3. **`random_strength=0.5` versus 0.0.** Historical source:
   `basketball_random_strength_result.md`. Agreement requires noisy-nonlinear
   ratio `<= 0.999` and more wins than losses.
4. **`random_strength=1.0` versus 0.5.** Same source. Agreement requires that
   1.0 is not a decisive improvement over 0.5: ratio `>= 0.999` or no win
   majority.
5. **Local `linear_leaves=True` versus constant leaves.** Historical sources:
   `smooth_linear_leaves_development_result.md` and
   `basketball_linear_leaves_result.md`. On `smooth_linear`, agreement
   requires ratio `<= 0.99` and wins on at least two thirds of datasets.
6. **Global `linear_residual=True` versus local linear leaves.** Historical
   source: `smooth_linear_leaves_development_result.md`. Agreement requires
   global/local ratio `>= 1.01` and more losses than wins on `smooth_linear`.
7. **`ts_permutations=4` versus 1.** Historical source:
   `tabarena_regression_multisplit_ablation.md`. Apply the original rejection
   logic on `categorical_regression`: agreement means aggregate improvement
   is below 0.5% or at least one dataset regresses by more than 0.5%.
8. **Forced `ordered_boosting=True` versus the scalar-regression default.**
   Historical source: `ordered_boosting_policy_check.md`. Agreement means
   aggregate improvement is below 0.5% or at least one ordinary-regression
   dataset regresses by more than 2%.
9. **Frozen speed-oriented core profile** (`l2_leaf_reg=1`,
   `max_bins=128`, `learning_rate=0.1`) versus current product defaults.
   Historical source: `tabarena_regression_remaining9_confirmation.md`.
   Agreement means the profile fails at least one original broad gate:
   aggregate improvement below 0.5%, a dataset regression above 0.5%, or a
   split regression above 2%.

The first six arms use the RMSE control unless an explicit comparator is named.
Student-t public predictions are its location head. No post-result slice,
threshold, arm, seed, version, suite, or comparator change is allowed.

## Adoption gate and disposition

SynthGen is adopted as a mechanism probe only if:

1. all generator, provenance, golden, split, and execution checks pass;
2. every declared slice is nonempty and contains at least eight datasets;
3. earned canaries pass their predeclared ceiling and no-variance checks;
4. at least seven of the nine ledger decisions agree; and
5. no protected outcome source was accessed.

A score below 7/9 closes this port as an adoption candidate. A passing score
does not validate future mechanisms; it only permits SynthGen to rank ideas
before basketball and fresh real-data gates. Raw results, exact source hashes,
environment, per-coordinate metrics, slice membership, scorecard, and the
pass/fail decision must be committed without rerunning or retuning.
