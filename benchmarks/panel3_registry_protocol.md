# Panel 3: dual-candidate confirmation and one lockbox shot

> **Status:** prospective protocol. Target access, model fitting,
> outcome inspection, and promotion are authorized only by the sequenced,
> immutable artifacts and validation gates defined below; this document alone
> never authorizes any of them.

## Purpose and evidence boundary

Panel 3 is the one prospective Tier-D adjudication of two already-defined
DarkoFit automatic policies:

1. the T5 composite policy; and
2. the standalone 5%-guarded numeric cross-feature policy.

They are two candidates, not one nested result and not a post-outcome menu.
Each is compared with the same current-default control and must pass its own
complete decision rule. ChimeraBoost 0.15.0 and CatBoost's product default are
same-machine descriptive comparators only. They cannot rescue, veto, rank, or
select either DarkoFit candidate.

The registry opens the still-sealed CTR23 lockbox once. Lockbox tasks exposed
after its original freeze are ineligible: the FIFA lineage was later used as
a T5/catalog alarm, Moneyball now appears in the bound ChimeraBoost exposure
catalog, and Socmob is explicitly analyzed in ChimeraBoost 0.15.0's
`HIGHCARD_PLAN.md`. The remaining six declared lockbox lineages may be used
only through this protocol. Six source-reviewed new lineages complete the
12-task panel.

This is an outcome-blind but eligibility-aware design. Preflight and registry
construction may inspect metadata, schemas, source descriptions, target
type/finiteness, opaque target hashes, dataset fingerprints, target-free
feature transformations, and the feature-only inputs needed to construct
splits. They may freeze official indices or explicit target-free split
indices. They must not compute target distribution statistics, fit a
candidate or control, score a prediction, or inspect any candidate/control
outcome.

Before H1, Parquet-footer minimum/maximum statistics for the target columns of
Mercari task 363377, Google QA task 363370, and Sberbank task 363495 were
inadvertently observed during structural review. Those three lineages are
permanently ineligible for Panel 3. The declaration's exact
`pre_h1_target_statistic_exclusions` ledger records the exposure class and
mechanical replacements without retaining the observed statistic values:
Garment Productivity task 360993, Sensory task 359931, and Baseball Hitter
task 4851, respectively. The replacements were already source-reviewed
reserves, were fixed before H1, and have had no target value or target
statistic inspected. No excluded task may remain in a candidate, related-task
set, ordinal map, power slot, reserve path, or final registry.

## Target-finiteness preauthorization

The protocol, declarations, candidate contract, preflight builder, registry

- a nonempty one-dimensional target;
- exact row binding to the dataset fingerprint;

- no Python or NumPy complex values; and
- no NaN, positive infinity, negative infinity, masked value, or overflow.

The create-only preflight artifact records pass/fail eligibility, exact

candidate/control outcome was persisted. A technical failure, integrity drift,

score, or candidate contrast.

Before the first target-column materialization or inspection, the preflight
must also reproduce the exact three-coordinate T5 size-gate applicability
vector for every power-authorized primary slot. It uses official split
indices, target-free custom-split feature projections, or (only when the
entire dataset is below the gate) the dataset row count as a strict upper
bound. OpenML may download and cache one Parquet container that physically
includes the target column; its API has no remote column-projection endpoint.
The structural pass projects only the declared feature columns, never decodes
or materializes the target column, and computes no target value, hash, or
statistic. The create-only preflight artifact records that precise boundary.
Any task, lineage, stratum, split-dimension, train-size, or applicability drift

## Exact arms

The complete constructor and selection contract is
`panel3_candidate_contract.json`.

### Current-default control

Fit `DarkoRegressor(random_state=4, thread_count=6)` on all outer-training
rows. This is the sole quality, cost, and memory denominator.

### T5 composite policy

Use the exact policy frozen in `t5_composite_registry_protocol.md`: the
2,000-row size gate, deterministic 20% inner validation, 10,000 rounds,
learning rate 0.1, L2 3, 128 bins, one target-stat permutation, automatic tree
mode, 100-round tree-mode auditions, full-budget constant/linear selection
only in CatBoost tree mode, declared complete ordinals, top-six numeric
diff/product features, the 5% cross guard, the 0.5% whole-challenger guard, and
full-outer-training refit at the selected resolved learning rate and exact
best-prefix length. Every decline must byte-match a separate current-default
full-data fit.

The declaration contains an explicit ordinal entry for every candidate task.
All 17 entries are empty because source review found no complete ordinal map
that could be frozen without guesswork. Missing entries are invalid; an empty
entry is an explicit decision, not a fallback.

### Guarded cross-feature policy

Use the exact source mechanism in `smooth_cross_features_protocol.md` and the
5% margin frozen in `smooth_cross_margin_analysis.json`: CatBoost-style
depth-6 trees, 2,000 rounds, learning rate 0.1, L2 1, 128 bins, minimum child
hessian 1, full-budget constant/linear selection, top-six numeric features by
split gain, all unordered pair differences and products, and crossed
engagement only when crossed validation RMSE is at most 0.95 times uncrossed
validation RMSE. Refit the selected lane and exact best prefix on all
outer-training rows. A cross decline is the uncrossed validation winner; it is
not silently replaced by the composite candidate.

For every arm, categorical columns are the union of OpenML's declared
categorical flags and columns whose loaded dtype is nonnumeric. Several
real-world uploads report zero categorical flags despite object columns;
flags alone are not an acceptable resolver. The registry freezes the resolved
categorical names after applying the exact feature policy, and every runner
must reproduce that ordered list and feature attestation exactly.

## Multiplicity-safe Tier-D decision

Both candidates first face the prospectively frozen two-candidate
power screen. If both are retained, each uses a Bonferroni one-sided 97.5%
hierarchical-bootstrap upper bound (per-candidate alpha 0.025). If exactly one
survives that screen and the separately frozen singleton recalculation, it
alone uses a one-sided 95% upper bound (alpha 0.05). Post-calibration
discretion and post-fresh-outcome winner selection are prohibited.

For each candidate/control outer-test RMSE ratio, equal-weight datasets after
geometric reduction across the three coordinates:

1. aggregate ratio `<= 0.995`;
2. the power-decision-bound hierarchical-bootstrap upper bound `<= 1.002`
   (one-sided 97.5% for two retained candidates or 95% for one);
3. least-favorable leave-one-dataset-out ratio `<= 0.998`;
4. worst-dataset ratio `<= 1.005`;
5. equal-dataset total fit-seconds ratio `<= 6.0`, worst-dataset fit ratio
   `<= 12.0`, equal-dataset repeated prediction ratio `<= 1.5`, and
   equal-dataset peak-RSS ratio `<= 2.5`; and
6. complete finite execution, exact source/raw/spool integrity, no imputation,
   and no protocol deviation.

The hierarchical bootstrap resamples 12 datasets with replacement and, for
each sampled dataset occurrence, samples three coordinates with replacement.
It uses seed `20260717` and 100,000 replicates. Each replicate therefore
contains 36 coordinate draws, including repeated dataset occurrences rather
than one fold draw per occurrence. The create-only power decision binds the
percentile before fresh target access: 97.5 for two retained candidates or 95
for a retained singleton. Win counts and comparison with ChimeraBoost/CatBoost
are descriptive only.

## Design-time power and current authorization blocker

Power is candidate-specific. Reusing the composite candidate's effect pool
for the guarded-cross candidate would assume the very transfer that panel 3
is meant to test. Sampling only the three datasets that nominated guarded
crosses would also condition on prior winners.

The first draft nevertheless made both mistakes. It sampled all 12 panel
lineages exchangeably from the 15-profile T5 component pool and gave the
three-winner guarded-cross pool a simulated probability of 1.0. Source review
found two pre-outcome defects:

- energy efficiency, forest fires, garment productivity, sensory, wheat,
  USNEWS colleges, and baseball hitter have fewer than 2,000 outer-training
  rows on every coordinate, so the composite is prospectively guaranteed to
  return an exact current-default no-op there;
  and
- the repository has no broad exact-policy guarded-cross census—only the
  three lineages selected to nominate it.

The binding provisional sensitivity uses NumPy's default RNG, seed
`20260717`, 200,000 simulations, and Bonferroni normal quantile
`1.959963984540054`. Five empirical T5-pool draws plus seven fixed size-gate
ties pass all four quality gates in `58893 / 200000 = 0.294465` simulations.
An optimistic safe-decline guarded-cross sensitivity that combines the three
observed nominees with 12 assumed exact non-nominee ties passes in
`94588 / 200000 = 0.47294`. Their Fréchet joint lower bound is `0`.
These diagnostics are not authorization evidence, and all are below the 0.80
floor.

Before this protocol can authorize a fresh outcome, the development-only
`panel3_cross_power_calibration_protocol.md` must run the exact current
control, exact composite, and exact standalone guarded-cross policies over all
39 predeclared coordinates of the already-spent 13-task TabArena regression
panel. Its effect pool must retain every engagement, decline, tie, loss, and
size-gated no-op. The already-frozen
`panel3_power_design_contract.json` and
`panel3_power_design_protocol.md` then consume the 39 complete triplets and
simulate the actual hierarchical-bootstrap decision—never a normal-z proxy.
Both the power point estimate and its preregistered one-sided Wilson lower
bound must clear 0.80. The deterministic Bonferroni-then-singleton retention
rule is binding; no joint-both-pass probability is required. A candidate that
does not survive is removed before target access and remains
opt-in/research-only.

The create-only decision is source-bound to the calibration's outcome-blind H1
freeze. Target preflight recomputes it from the bound raw/spool/summary once.
It authorizes registry construction only when all twelve exact primary tasks
are target-eligible. Any reserve substitution invalidates the transported
power calculation and stops the campaign.

## Execution, raw boundary, and no-rerun rule

Before any fit, the completed runner must validate every registry/source hash
and reproduce the complete registry through the deterministic builder before
creating the retained-candidate plan: 36 coordinates times current default,
one or two preregistered DarkoFit candidates, ChimeraBoost, and CatBoost. This comparison covers every
task row, selected/reserve decision, feature policy, frozen split, declaration,
and target-preflight binding; a registry's self-hash alone is not sufficient.
Each isolated worker warms its engine, records fitted metadata, exact
prediction hashes, RMSE, fit/predict seconds, peak RSS, categorical resolution,
selected lanes, resolved learning rate, best iteration, and stop/refit reason.
The parent applies the analyzer's complete result and fitted-metadata contract
before publishing or reusing a create-only hash-bound spool record. It applies
the complete raw validator, including reopening every spool, before publishing
the immutable raw artifact. Resume reuses validated records byte for byte. A
separate analyzer alone computes gates.

Every create-only output must remain below the repository root. The complete
ancestor chain is validated without following symlinks before any model fit,
and publication uses a held directory descriptor so an ancestor swap cannot
redirect a spool or artifact.

The analyzer's archival mode validates the registry, raw artifact, execution
plan, task/split invariants, measurements, and embedded hashes without opening
later live protocol, candidate, preflight, evidence, source, environment, or
spool files. Live-file verification remains mandatory for the canonical
analysis. Archival mode exists only to reproduce an already-published immutable
raw artifact after the repository evolves; it cannot authorize execution.

`run_panel3_confirmation.py` and `analyze_panel3_confirmation.py` implement
the complete isolated-worker, immutable-spool, source-recheck, and analysis
boundary. Their presence alone does not authorize a model fit. The registry
must not set `confirmation_run_authorized=true` until the completed sources
have been reviewed and committed, a fresh target preflight binds those exact
source bytes, the create-only registry is built from that preflight, and every
registry/source integrity check passes.

Once any selected-task arm is scored, all 12 selected lineages are spent.
No task replacement, gate change, candidate change, outcome-dependent rerun,
or post-hoc single-candidate selection is permitted. Repairs may resume only
from prediction-identical spool records. Any model-affecting repair requires a
new candidate and new outcome-unseen evidence.
